Paths to Citizenship

There are many common paths to citizenship. Some are easier than others. See the following below:

  1. Spouse of a U.S. citizen

    • You are qualified to begin the Naturalization process provided that you have lived for three years in the U.S. prior to applying.

  2. Family Members in the Military

    • Those in the military can receive fee waivers if they fill out the following form: https://www.uscis.gov/policy-manual/volume-12-part-i-chapter-5

  3. Children of Citizens/Permanent Residents

    • Ex. As a permanent resident, you need to have lived in the U.S. for 5 years before you can apply for the N-400 form.

  4. Older Residents

    • If you are 75 yr or Older, you can be exempted from fees.

  5. Unaccompanied & Undocumented Minors

    • For minors of undocumented parents, there is a way to lawful immigration status.

    • These minors must...

      1. Be under 18 years old
      2. Have one parent (birth or adopted) that is a U.S. citizen (birth or naturalization)

    • If they meet all of these requirements, then they should fill out the N-600 form instead of the N-400 and follow the instructions detailed inthe Naturalization: Path To Citizenship tab.

      1. Link To N-600 Form: https://www.uscis.gov/sites/default/files/document/forms/n-600.pdf
